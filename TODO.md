## Issues identified from recent changes (to fix)
- ✅ **Distance-from-base calculation ignores Z**: The policy input uses `distance_from_base_m = math.sqrt(x * x + y * y)` omitting the Z coordinate, which may underestimate distance for workspace violations. Fix: include Z in Euclidean distance: `math.sqrt(x*x + y*y + z*z)`.
- ✅ **Memory leak in _trajectory_store**: The in-memory trajectory staging store never removes entries except on successful confirmation or explicit cleanup, risking unbounded growth. Fix: add a timeout-based cleanup (e.g., remove entries older than N seconds) or clear on `confirm_execution` failure and preview timeout.
- ✅ **Policy input schema mismatch**: The `policy_input` dict includes `velocity` and `distance_from_base` but the PolicyEngine may expect different field names or units. Fix: verify the exact schema expected by `policy_engine.evaluate_trajectory` and align keys/units accordingly (e.g., ensure `velocity` is in mm/s if policy expects that).
- ✅ **Pseudo velocity metric unclear**: The derived `velocity_metric` is a placeholder and may not reflect real-world velocity used by policies, causing incorrect policy decisions. Fix: replace with a meaningful velocity estimate (e.g., max joint speed) or document that policy currently ignores this field.
- ✅ **Missing validation for trajectory length = 1**: The velocity calculation divides by `max(1, len(trajectory)-1)`, which is safe but when `len(trajectory)==1` the velocity metric becomes zero; this may be intentional but should be checked against policy expectations.
- ✅ **Audit log duplication**: In `state_manager.py`, the `execution_lifecycle` field is added after `payload_hash` but may duplicate existing logging; ensure it does not break downstream consumers.
- ✅ **Unused import**: `import math` is used, but ensure no stray imports remain.
